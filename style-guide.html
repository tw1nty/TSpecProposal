01 / Colors

A four-color brand.

Most pages are essentially black-on-cream. Orange is rare and decisive — when it appears, it owns the moment. Dope-black sets up high-contrast bands between cream sections.

02 / Semantic Surfaces

Surfaces with intent.

Raw hexes are nicknames; semantic surfaces are job descriptions. Each surface declares its background, the foreground that pairs with it, the borders that work on it, and the moments it's appropriate to use. Reach for these in product code — never `#F43C00` directly.

Default Page Canvas--bg

Cream — the warm default.

The page starts here. Cards float above it, photography sits on it, body type lives on it. 80% of the brand's surface area.

fg: --fg1 / #000 muted: --fg2 / 30% black border: --border / 10% black surface above: --surface / #FFF
Card / Elevated Surface--surface

White — the lift surface.

Cards, form fields, modal sheets. Always sits above cream or grey, never as the page itself. Keeps photography crisp and forms calm.

fg: --fg1 / #000 muted: --fg2 / 30% black border: --border / 10% black accent: --accent / orange
Alt Section Background--bg-alt

Grey — quiet separation.

Use sparingly to delineate a long page into chapters without going full dark. Pairs with white cards for double-elevation.

fg: --fg1 / #000 surface above: --surface / #FFF border: --border
Inverted Section--bg-dark

Dope-black — the foil.

Testimonials, dark-band CTAs, footer. Foreground flips to white. Borders go translucent-white. Orange still works as the accent.

fg: --fg-on-dark muted: --fg-on-dark-muted border: --border-dark accent: --accent
Accent Surface--accent

Orange — one moment per view.

A single CTA. A single accent dot. The 404 hero. Foreground pairs with white. Never the canvas of a full page or large card.

on-accent: --on-accent / #FFF no body text on this
Accent Tint (derived)color-mix(--accent, --bg)

Orange tint — soft callout.

For badges, "new" pills, hover wells. Derived, not a base token — generate via color-mix(in oklab, var(--accent) 18%, var(--bg)).

fg: dark orange #5A1500 use: badges, soft hover

07 / Voice

Bold. Direct. A little cocky.

Imperative phrases stacked into vertical poems. Numbers when they help the brag. Em dashes. Smart quotes. Never emoji. We talk like an agency that wants to win the pitch.

Hero pattern

Growth Hacking. Push Limits. Pull Together. and Unite the Culture.

Period-terminated fragments. Stacked vertically. Lower-case "and" is intentional.

Comparative brag

Why convert at 2% when you can convert at 20%?

Numbers in service of the punchline. Question form, no question apology.

Subscription strapline

Delivering digital experiences through versatile subscription

Service-pitch register. Confident. No verbs of obligation.

Footer hours

Mo—Fr · 9am—6pm

Em dash, abbreviated weekdays. Clean and editorial, not clinical.

Do / Don't

Do

Stack short, period-terminated phrases.

"Push Limits. Pull Together." Each line earns its own breath. Tracking at -4%, line-height 100%.

Don't

Write smooth marketing prose.

"We are committed to delivering best-in-class results that empower our valued clients." Off-brand. Generic. No.

Do

Use cream as the default canvas.

Black ink on warm #EEE9E3. Photography full-bleed inside 28px-rounded frames.

Don't

Reach for gradients or emoji.

No bluish-purple gradients. No 🚀. No glassmorphism. Color blocking does the work.

Do

Reserve orange for one moment per view.

One CTA in orange. One accent dot. One link hover. The orange owns the moment because it's rare.

Don't

Color body text orange.

Orange on body type fights legibility and dilutes the accent. Keep body in black, accents tactical.
